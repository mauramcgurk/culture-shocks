About Us

Conventional wisdom says that studying abroad is like living a fairy tale: making friends all over the world, learning a new language, even experiencing romance in an exotic locale.

The very definition of studying abroad is being far from home, and that comes with the challenges of constructing new support networks, learning a new language and daily customs, and coping without the comforts of home. What was in the past known loosely as "homesickness" is more correctly termed "culture shock" and takes into account the predictable patterns and varying causes. It often comes as a surprise that there is a second adjustment period, the Reverse Culture Shock, that occurs after return to the home culture, when support systems are again disrupted.

The Stages of Culture Shock

Culture Shocks is an app designed by exchange students, for exchange students, to help them deal with the stresses of living abroad.
